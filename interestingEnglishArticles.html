目标等于自由

By Steve Pavlina

如果你不知道你自己的目标，你也不会花太多时间在你现在做的事情上面。什么事会让你停止现在的工作并开始做其他的事呢？
三种情况：

  1. 你自身的需求
  2. 他人的需求
  3. 他人的目标

如果你不知道自己的目标，你的能力水平也将被限制在满足自己的需求上，最多会让你有变得更贪婪的可能。这样的生活不会让人特别满意...如果你不相信我你可以自己花几年试试，然后看看镜子中那个没有激情的自己。当你为和其他人一起工作，你会尽力去满足别人的需求和贪婪的本性，你的老板，你的顾客，你的投资人等等。即使在非工作时间，你也会去满足那些希望你去看电视和购物的广告主们的渴望。其次，尽管赞助人会以礼物作为有趣项目的打赏，向给狗狗打赏几块骨头一样，但仍不能让人满足。这种生活最终会让你站起来大喊：“这一切的最终目标是什么”。但如果你真的这么做了，你只会被白眼。
如果你足够幸运，你将有机会跟你有相同明确目标的组织和个人一起工作。虽然不会被告知具体的目标时什么。除了意外和机会，你无法肯定的选择跟你有相同目标的人结盟，即使有机率也很低。所以有一个不错的机会是你可以认真的为一个你不是很同意的目标工作。比如，参军，你将被安排去实现宏大的目标，但具体是什么？很可能的是在这些情况下，你将被赋予一些你不回主动去选择的目标让你去执行。花费一生做这些事会让人得到满足吗？不可能，但至少这对那些不喜欢思考的人来说是一个不错的途径。一条别人已经帮你想过的路。（对于缺乏考虑的行为而言好处大很多）。

Purpose = Freedom

By Steve Pavlina

It’s fair to say that if you don’t know your purpose in life, you won’t be spending much time working on it. So what will you end up doing with your working time instead? Three things:

  1. Working on your needs,
  2. Working on other people's needs,
  3. Working on other people's purposes.

If you don’t know your purpose, the limit of the work you do for yourself will be stuck at the level of need, which at best has the potential to grow into greed. Not particularly fulfilling spending your whole working life this way… Try it yourself for a few decades if you don’t believe me, and then look at the passionless shell that stares back at you from your mirror. As you work with/for other people, most likely you’ll be putting lots of effort into satisfying other people’s needs and greeds: your boss, your customers, your company’s investors, etc. Even in your free time, you’ll be working to fulfill the desires of advertisers who want you to watch TV and buy stuff. Again, not particularly satisfying, although you may be thrown a few bones by your benefactors, such as the “gift” of working on some interesting projects. This kind of life will ultimately make you want to stand up and shout, “What exactly is the point of all of this?” But if you actually do that, you’ll only get blank stares in return. There is no point.
Now if you’re very lucky, you may get the chance to work for someone or some organization which is itself focused on achieving a conscious purpose. However, there’s no telling what that purpose might be. If you don’t know your own purpose, you can’t consciously choose to work for someone whose purpose aligns with yours except by accident or chance, and the odds of alignment are low. So there’s a good chance you’ll be working hard to achieve a purpose you don’t agree with. For example, if you join the military, you may be put to use to achieve some big purpose, but what exactly will it be? Most likely, in such situations you’ll be given a purpose to achieve that isn’t what you’d choose consciously for yourself. Fulfilling to spend your whole life this way? Not likely, but it’s at least a decent path for people who don’t like to think much — others will take care of all the thinking for you (and benefit greatly from all your thoughtless doing).
